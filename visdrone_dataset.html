VisDrone Dataset

A large-scale benchmark for drone-based computer vision tasks

Overview

VisDrone is one of the most comprehensive benchmarks for drone-based computer vision tasks, including object detection and tracking. The dataset was collected by the AISKYEYE team at Lab of Machine Learning and Data Mining, Tianjin University, China.

Dataset Statistics

  • Captured by: Various drone-mounted cameras
  • Geographic coverage: 14 different cities across China
  • Environments: Both urban and country settings
  • Object categories: Pedestrians, cars, bicycles, tricycles, and more
  • Attributes: Scene visibility, object class, and occlusion information
  • Altitude range: 15-180 meters
  • Weather conditions: Various weather and lighting scenarios

Object Detection in Images (VisDrone-DET)

  • Trainset: 1.44 GB
  • Valset: 0.07 GB
  • Testset-dev: 0.28 GB (Ground truth available)
  • Testset-challenge: 0.28 GB

Object Detection in Videos (VisDrone-VID)

  • Trainset: 7.53 GB
  • Valset: 1.49 GB
  • Testset-dev: 2.14 GB (Ground truth available)
  • Testset-challenge: 2.70 GB

Single-Object Tracking (VisDrone-SOT)

  • Trainset: ~20.37 GB (split into two parts)
  • Valset: 1.29 GB
  • Testset-dev: 11.27 GB (Ground truth available)
  • Testset-challenge: ~34.71 GB (split into two parts)

Multi-Object Tracking (VisDrone-MOT)

  • Trainset: 7.53 GB
  • Valset: 1.48 GB
  • Testset-dev: 2.14 GB (Ground truth available)
  • Testset-challenge: 2.70 GB

Strengths and Limitations

Strengths

  • Exceptional scale and diversity
  • Supports multiple tasks (detection, tracking, counting)
  • Well-documented with established benchmarks
  • Regular updates and challenges
  • Realistic drone-captured footage

Limitations

  • Primarily focused on urban environments
  • Limited to visible spectrum imagery
  • Annotation density varies across dataset
  • Some classes have limited representation

Characteristics for Drone Deployment

  • Collected using various drone platforms (different models)
  • Captured under various weather and lighting conditions
  • Includes sparse and crowded scenes
  • Provides annotations for common objects of interest in aerial surveillance
